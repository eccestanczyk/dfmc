XP & LEVELING

Other Systems › XP & Leveling

Two-phase leveling built for an autoplay climb: a gentle journey to level 66 that opens the whole tower fast, then a steep geometric endgame that carries hundreds of hours.

The two phases are continuous at level 67. XP per kill is flat in the enemy's level, so the battle counts below follow directly from the requirement curve.

The curve is deliberately back-loaded: about 75% of all leveling time sits in levels 90–100, and 50% in the last five levels. Total to max: ~20,300 battles ≈ 680 h. Reaching level 67 — where wild Stage 3 creatures appear — takes only ~885 battles (~30 h).

Player level (uncapped)

Separate from creature levels, class levels, and character level. The player level is an account-wide marker of total time in the tower — it is purely cosmetic today and grants nothing: no stats, no unlocks, no gear requirements. It exists so that the endgame keeps counting, and so that a future system has a natural place to hook into.

PropertyRule
XP sourceEvery point of XP the account earns, from every battle, in every league. Player XP is never split — it is the raw total.
Curve, levels 1–100Identical to the creature / class curve: 10 × L^1.75, then 234 × L × 1.16^(L−67).
Curve, levels 101+Flat. Every level past 100 costs a fixed PLAYER_FLAT_XP = 639,723 XP — the level-90 requirement, held constant. No cap; the counter never stops. Kept as a single tunable constant rather than derived from the curve, so the vanity pace can be retuned without touching the leveling model.
Hardcore deathPlayer level is not lost. Characters, creatures, items, and class XP are wiped; the player level is the one number that survives, because it measures the account, not the run.
GrantsNothing. Deliberately inert.

Pacing, past 100: at level-100 enemies (10 × 100 = 1,000 XP per kill) each player level is ~640 battles ≈ 21 h of autoplay — roughly one level a fortnight for a steady player, so the counter visibly ticks over instead of freezing. Reaching player level 100 itself takes ~21.6 M XP; every level after is a flat ~640 k grind, climbing linearly forever instead of exploding. Continuing the level-100 requirement (3.1 M, ~105 h/level) was rejected as too slow to feel alive.

Who receives XP

  • End-of-battle XP goes to every creature in the party and the avatar, not only the participants.
  • Dying in battle does not forfeit XP — a fainted creature or downed avatar still receives its full share.
  • Each recipient's XP is then scaled by the level gap to the defeated enemy. See Level Penalty for the full curve.
Hardcore implication: death wipes a character to level 1. The relevel to 67 is ~30 h of autoplay, but a death at level 95+ costs a dedicated player weeks — high stakes by design. See Death & Leagues.
