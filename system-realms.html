DEATH & REALMS

Other Systems › Death & Realms

Every account holds one character in every unlocked realm, each starting at level 1. The character slot is permanent — death never removes or moves it. What death costs depends on the realm. There is no crypt panel in this version: a dead hardcore character's holdings travel as a Bequest and are claimed from a single inbox. Everything — gear, creatures, eggs, Soul Gems — is an item in one unified inventory.

The four realms

Eternal

Soft death: stay on the floor, streak resets, character unchanged. Receives every Hardcore death as a claimable Bequest (see below), plus Seasonal contents at season end.

Hardcore

On death the character is wiped to level 1 and stays in Hardcore. Its entire loadout — every item, every creature, one Soul Gem per class it had XP in — is sealed into a Bequest and sent to Eternal to be claimed.

Seasonal

Soft death like Eternal. Timed realm; at season end the character migrates to Eternal and its holdings are delivered there.

HC Seasonal

On death, wiped to level 1 in place; the loadout is sealed into a Bequest. At season end all HC Seasonal Bequests are delivered to Hardcore to be claimed there.

Soul Gems

When a hardcore character is wiped, one Soul Gem is generated per class it had any XP in and placed inside that death's Bequest. Rank scales with the XP amount. A Soul Gem is an item like any other; consuming it grants that XP to the surviving character's matching class — softening a wipe without erasing the loss.

The Bequest inbox

Hardcore deaths do not silently deposit loot into a vault. Each death produces one Bequest — a single sealed record of that character at the moment it fell — delivered to the receiving realm's inbox to be claimed by hand.

The icon

  • A gravestone with a satchel resting in front of it sits in the top bar of the receiving realm, positioned between the menu dropdown and the Speed-order button.
  • It is hidden entirely when nothing is pending — the bar looks normal until a death occurs.
  • When Bequests are waiting it appears with a count badge. The badge counts Bequests (deaths), not items — “3” means three characters to review.
  • The icon lives only in realms that receive Bequests: Eternal (from Hardcore) and Hardcore (from HC Seasonal, at season end). It never appears in a realm that only sends.

What a Bequest holds

One Bequest is one death, kept whole. Bequests never merge into a shared pile — multiple deaths stack as discrete, individually-labelled entries.

The claim popup

  • Opens to a list of Bequests, newest first. Each is a collapsed row: ☠ Paladin · Lv 87 · Floor 91 · 2h ago · 14 items · 3 gems · 5 creatures. Expanding a row reveals the full contents.
  • Claim per Bequest empties that one; Claim All works the whole queue. Claimed contents flow into the single unified inventory.
  • Claiming is atomic per item, not per Bequest. If the inventory fills mid-claim, the items that fit are taken and the remainder stays inside the Bequest, which remains in the queue showing only what is left.

How stacking is handled

The inbox itself is uncapped; nothing is ever destroyed for lack of room. Die ten times at level 90 and ten labelled Bequests wait patiently, each claimable when space allows. The only pressure the player ever feels is “my inventory is full” — never “my inbox overflowed and I lost the gear.” Because claims are per-item and the queue is unbounded, stacking is always safe and never punitive: the unified inventory is the single capacity gate, and a partially-claimed Bequest simply lingers with its remainder until the player makes room.

One character at a time

  • The game simulates one realm character at a time. Switching realms switches the active character and pauses the previous one entirely.
  • There is no offline progression — being in-game is the only way to advance. Economy is balanced in battles for this one active character (~30 battles/hour on autoplay).
  • The character slot is permanent in every realm; even a hardcore wipe leaves the character in place at level 1.
Hardcore stakes scale with the leveling curve: a relevel to 67 is ~30 h, but a death at level 95+ costs weeks. See XP & Leveling for the full curve. In-battle defeat (not permadeath) is covered under Win Streak & Floors.
