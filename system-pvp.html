PVP, TIMERS & PERSISTENCE

Other Systems › PvP, Timers & Persistence

Ranked 3v3 (avatar + 2 creatures per side), best of 3, team locked at match start, double-blind simultaneous move selection. This page defines the PvP screen, the move timers, how battles survive crashes and disconnects, hardcore quit-out rules, and the leaderboards.

The PvP Arena screen

Entered from the main menu (PVP ARENA, between Adventure Log and Game Mode). One screen, four regions:

RegionContents
Rank card (top left)Current rank, rating number, season W/L, and placement progress. Ranks reuse the game's own ladder language: Worn → Void — the ten grade names as ten rank tiers, top of ladder is Void.
Team lock (center)Three sockets: avatar + 2 creatures. This is the roster the queue locks; creatures sitting in breeding pens are ineligible. A team-power summary line sits below the sockets. Locked for the whole Bo3 — no sideboarding between games.
Queue button (bottom center)One large FIND MATCH button; while queuing it becomes a cancel with elapsed time. Matchmaking pairs by rating, band widening the longer the wait.
Right railTwo tabs: Leaderboard (top 100 + your position) and History (recent matches, each opening a full replay — replays are free because battles are deterministic logs, see Persistence).

Move timers

Both sides pick all their moves for the round simultaneously; timers exist to bound the round, not each unit. The design follows the banked chess-clock model competitive simulators converged on, sized to our 10–16-turn fights.

TimerValueRule
Round timer (PvP)30 sBoth players pick within it. Resolution/animation time does not count.
Personal bank (PvP)90 sDrawn from only after the 30 s round timer is spent, chess-clock style. Never refills during a game; resets between games of the Bo3.
On expiry—The autoplay AI picks for you — the same AI that runs PvE grinding, not "first move in the list." Losing time costs quality, not the game outright.
Match cap (PvP)30 roundsHard cap at ~2× the tuned fight length. Tiebreak in order: units remaining → total remaining HP% → cumulative damage dealt.
PvENo timerMove timers are a PvP-integrity tool; against the AI there is nothing to protect and autoplay already exists for players who want speed. PvE battles instead get full crash-safe resume (below).

Persistence — battles survive anything

Every battle is a deterministic replay: initial state + per-round RNG seed + committed move log. That single decision buys crash recovery, reconnects, replays, and hardcore integrity all at once.

RuleDetail
Save cadenceOnce per round: both sides' committed choices plus the round's RNG seed are persisted before the resolution is shown. One small write per round.
PvE crashRelaunch resumes the battle at the top of the current round — same state, same seed. Nothing is lost and nothing rerolls.
Anti-scumBecause the seed is committed before results render, killing the game after seeing a bad roll and reloading replays the identical roll. There is no outcome to reroll, ever.
PvP authorityPvP state lives server-side (Unity Relay + UGS per the tech stack); clients are views. Reconnecting re-fetches the authoritative state.

Disconnects

CaseRule
PvP disconnectThe match does not pause and timers keep burning — pausing on disconnect is a documented exploit vector (players firewall-drop to buy free thinking time). When your round timer + bank expire, the autoplay AI plays your side, exactly as on a timeout.
Reconnect windowReconnect at any point and control returns at the next round. After 5 consecutive fully-AI rounds, the game is forfeited — long enough to survive a router reboot, short enough that a rage-quit cannot hold the opponent hostage.
PvE crash / quitIdentical outcomes: resume at the current round on next launch. A battle in progress can be resumed but never abandoned.

Hardcore & Alt+F4

The classic hardcore failure mode is the logout escape: kill the client the instant a fight goes wrong. Turn-based rounds close that door structurally.

RuleWhy it holds
Battles are resume-onlyAlt+F4, crash, and power loss are indistinguishable and all lead to the same place: the battle resumes at the top of the current round with the committed seed. Quitting never exits a battle.
Committed rounds standOnce both sides' moves are locked, resolution is deterministic and already persisted. If the round that kills the character has committed, the death happens on resume — watching it or not changes nothing.
No mid-round escape valueKilling the client before committing a move just returns the player to the same decision with the same information. In a turn-based game there is no "leave the server before the hit lands" — nothing advances without a committed round.
Hardcore PvPUses the standard PvP rules above; a hardcore PvP loss is a match loss, not a character death. Permadeath is a PvE-climb stake only.
