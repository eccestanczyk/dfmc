BREEDING

Other Systems › Breeding

Breeding is a continuous pen, not a transaction. A pair is placed in a pen and the pen is toggled on; from then on it eats Food minute by minute and fills a progress bar. When the bar completes, an egg drops into the inventory and the pen keeps going. Food is the only limiter — it has no other use in the game.

The pen grid

Six pens, shown as a 2×3 grid. Pen 1 is unlocked from the start. The first additional pen is bought with Food; the remaining four are DLC.

What extra pens actually buy. Not throughput — Food income caps that regardless of pen count. Extra pens buy parallelism: several different pairs, types, and grade-ladders running at once, and no idle time while you swap partners. A player with six pens and a player with two pens hatch the same number of Void eggs per hour; the six-pen player is also climbing five other lines while they do it.

Choosing a pair

The pen has two sockets, ♂ and ♀. Clicking a socket opens a full-screen roster picker — it leaves the pen grid rather than crowding it, so the 2×3 grid stays intact.

RuleDetail
GenderOne male, one female. Gender is a flat 50/50 roll at hatch and never changes.
Shared typeThe two parents must share at least one type. All three type slots count (primary, secondary, tertiary) — an overlap anywhere is enough.
Progressive filterThe first socket lists every creature of that gender. Once one parent is chosen, the second picker filters to compatible partners only: opposite gender, sharing a type, not already in another pen.
ExclusivityA creature can sit in only one pen at a time.
Battle lockoutA creature in a pen cannot be in the battle party. Breeding your best fighters means fighting without them.
Same lineAllowed.
Type scarcity is the real gate. Across the roster of 300, the median creature has 44 type-compatible species and the tightest has 11. The four three-creature types — Celestial, Demon, Chimera, Aberration — are a single line each, so they can only breed within their own line. Breeding rewards a broad collection, not a strong one.

Pair grade

Every rate in the system keys off one number: pair grade P.

P = floor( (grade of parent A + grade of parent B) / 2 )

Mismatched parents are punished by the floor: a Void paired with a Rare breeds as Abyssal (P = 7), not as Void. Climbing the grade ladder means lifting both parents.

The loop

StateBehaviour
Toggle ONThe pen drains Food_Per_Min(P) every minute and fills the bar. P is locked in at toggle-on.
Bar fullAn egg is sent to the inventory, the bar resets to zero, and the pen continues automatically as long as Food and inventory space remain.
Toggle OFFDrain stops. Progress is retained, not lost.
Egg inventory fullDrain stops, the bar freezes at its current fill. Nothing is consumed and nothing is lost. Clear an egg and it resumes.
Food hits zeroSame as above: drain stops, bar freezes, pen resumes the moment Food arrives.
Removing a parentResets that pen's meter to zero. This is what stops a player filling a bar on a cheap Worn pair and swapping in two Voids at 99%.
ClockSession time only. Pens tick while the game is running — the same clock that generates the Food they eat. No offline accrual.

Food income

Food comes from combat, and scales with what you killed — level and rarity both. Per defeated enemy:

Food = round( 2 × (1 + enemyLevel / 20) × RarityMult )

RarityMult:   C 1.0   UC 1.5   R 2.5   SR 4.0   SSR 7.0

Egg dismantling

Unwanted eggs dismantle back into Food, keyed to the egg's grade (not the pair that made it).

No breed-and-dismantle loop. Yields are tuned against the hatch-weight matrix so that the expected dismantle value of an egg is always 27–30% of what the pen spent making it — at every rung of the ladder, including the up-rolls. Breeding for Food is always a loss; dismantling is a refund on a bad roll, never an engine.

Egg grade output

The pen does not need a new matrix. The pair grade P indexes the existing row in codex/egg_hatch_weights.csv — the same matrix that later rolls the hatchling's grade from the egg's grade. Two Void parents roll a Void egg 78.1% of the time (14% Primordial, 5% Divine, and a long tail below), and that Void egg then rolls a Void creature 78.1% of the time: ~61% Void end-to-end, with a real chance of falling short at either step. The Hatchery grade-boost upgrade shifts the second roll upward.

Throughput ceiling

Food income — not pen count — is the hard cap on egg production.

Scenario (endgame, ~3,040 Food/h)Result
Void pair, any number of pens~1.06 eggs / hour, ceiling
Epic pairs~7.9 eggs / hour

Six pens, Worn through Legendary3,060 Food/h — almost exactly sustainable at floor 99

A single Void pen alone eats half of a maxed player's entire Food income. That is the intended shape: the top of the ladder is slow by construction, and no amount of pens buys past it.
