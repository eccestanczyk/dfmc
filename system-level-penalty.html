LEVEL PENALTY

Other Systems › Level Penalty

Every battle awards experience at the end. This page defines who receives it and how much is kept once the level gap between the recipient and the enemy it defeated is applied.

Who gets XP

  • XP is awarded to every creature in the party and the avatar — not only the creatures that fought.
  • Dying in battle does not forfeit XP. A fainted creature or a downed avatar still receives its full end-of-battle share.
  • The penalty is computed per recipient, from that recipient's own level versus the defeated enemy's level. A low-level benchwarmer carried through a high-level fight keeps almost all of its XP; an over-leveled veteran farming weak floors keeps very little.

The penalty curve

Let diff = enemyLevel − recipientLevel. Positive means the enemy is higher level (you are under-leveled); negative means the enemy is lower (you are over-leveled). The two directions use different, deliberately asymmetric curves.

Enemy higher — forgiving

No penalty at all up to +10. Past that it ramps linearly, reaching the 90% floor at +50 and staying there.

diff ≤ 10 → 0%
10 < diff < 50 → 90% × (diff−10)/40
diff ≥ 50 → 90%

Enemy lower — strict

Half the window, twice as steep. Grace only to −3, then a fast ramp hitting 90% by −25.

d = −diff
d ≤ 3 → 0%
3 < d < 25 → 90% × (d−3)/22
d ≥ 25 → 90%

diff +5 · penalty 0%
XP retained 100%
